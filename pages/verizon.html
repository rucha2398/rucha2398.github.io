The Problem

Within the Verizon ecosystem, there are dozens of apps for their customers. The client was looking to create a centralized dashboard for users to setup, view and manage all of their security settings. Over the months, we helped design a setup experience, dashboard experience, as well as an experience to view overall security stats and ratings based on your profile.

The client was looking for a dashboard and an easy way for users to set up their security settings.




The Research

Before starting this project, we took a step back to look at overarching themes to help guide our designs. Key findings were that copy needed to be minimal and easy to understand, the experience needed to be customized to fit the user‚Äôs needs and there needed to be transparency between each step so the user felt engaged during the process.




The Sketches

I researched existing security platforms, as well as other mobile apps that used similar interaction patterns for quizzes and smart setup experiences. Here are some of the very initial sketches I created.

The smart setup would consist of
* an introduction
* a series of questions to customize the primary user‚Äôs experience
* a transparent automated profile setup

The Flow

A very basic low-fidelity flow for this experience.




The Iterations

I iterated on the screens for many months. Every week I presented designs to the client for feedback. Over the months, new requirements appeared and the amount of flows grew exponentially.

The final smart setup flow consisted of three distinct portions:
* Creating profiles for all account users
* Setting up all personal and work devices and placing each device into a group (to optimize security settings)
* Applying security settings to all of these devices




The Challenges

Given time restraints and lack of data for security settings, we designed the first two portions of this smart setup process.
